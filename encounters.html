AC HD No. Attacks Damage Move No. Appearing Save As Treasure Type Morale XP

Mansquitos are small (~3' high) humanoid mosquitos who are found in high humidity areas with lots of stagnant water. After a successful attack, a mansquito will "sample" the victim's blood, restoring 2 points of lost HP. If the blood "tastes" right (75% for humans, 50% for lizardmen), the rest of the mansquito flock will descend on the victim and attempt to fly him away to their hive, where he will be drugged and placed in a marinading chamber the queen decides that he is finished (10% after the second day, rising 10% each day after that). Flocks do not go more than a day's travel away from the hive and only have a 10% chance of needing to rest before reaching it. The victim's comrades will find it easy to follow the flock, which moves in a straight line back home after prey suitable for the queen is captured.

Spirit
AC HD No. Attacks Damage Move No. Appearing Save As Treasure Type Morale XP
17 (m) 10 1 touch 1d8+special 30' 1 Fighter 10 E, N, O 10 1200

Spirits are undead and therefore immune to Sleep, Charm, and Hold spells. A spirit that hits a living target with its touch attack does 1d8 points of damage, and at the same time regenerates the same number of hit points. In addition, the victim loses 1 Constitution point. Long-lived or immortal combatants are allowed a saving throw vs. Death Ray to resist this effect, which must be rolled on each hit. Spirits may normally be hit only by magical weapons. However, if a ghost makes use of its touch attack, it becomes vulnerable to non-magical weapons until the beginning of the next round of combat.

Instead of attacking, a spirit may attempt to possess a living creature. This ability is similar to the Magic Jar spell (as if cast by a 10th level Magic-User), except that it does not require a receptacle. To use this ability, the spirit must be able to move into the target (so it is possible to avoid this attack by outrunning the ghost). The target can resist the attack with a successful save vs. Spells. A creature that successfully saves is immune to being possessed by that spirit for 24 hours. If the save fails, the spirit enters the target's body and controls it; control may be maintained until the spirit chooses to leave the victim's body, or until it is driven out by means of Remove Curse or Dispel Evil. While it is possessing a living creature, a spirit may not use any of its special abilities.
